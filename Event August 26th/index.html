Honoring Marsha P. Johnson: A Legacy of Liberation and Love (1945-1992)

This week, we celebrate the birth of Marsha P. Johnson (August 24, 1945) — a trailblazing activist, performer, and unapologetic advocate for LGBTQ+ rights. As a central figure in the Stonewall uprising and a co-founder of the Street Transvestite Action Revolutionaries (STAR), Marsha’s fearless voice uplifted those most marginalized in society, especially trans women of color.
 

MARSHA P. JOHNSON

Beyond her activism, Marsha embodied the power of joy as resistance. Her presence in New York’s LGBTQ+ community was vibrant, loving, and revolutionary. With flowers in her hair and courage in her heart, she challenged injustice with grace and grit.
As we honor her birthday, we remember that Marsha’s fight was not just for visibility, but for dignity, safety, and freedom for all. Her legacy lives on in today’s movements for queer and trans liberation—reminding us to show up, speak out, and care for one another boldly.
